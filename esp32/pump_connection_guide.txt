=====================================================
   PUMP CONNECTION GUIDE - ESP32 + Relay Control
          (Final Corrected Version)
=====================================================

PROBLEM:
  Both the pump and ESP32 are powered from the charge
  controller. How does the ESP32 control the pump?

ANSWER:
  The ESP32 does NOT power the pump. It sends a tiny
  control signal to a RELAY MODULE, which acts as an
  electrically controlled switch for the pump's own
  power line.

=====================================================
   OVERALL SYSTEM LAYOUT
=====================================================

  Solar Panel --> Charge Controller --> Battery
                         |
               +---------+---------+
               |                   |
         Buck Converter       LOAD Terminal
         (5V or 3.3V)       (direct 12V output)
               |                   |
             ESP32            Water Pump
                              (through relay)

  NOTE: The pump uses the LOAD terminal directly.
        Only the ESP32 needs a buck converter.

=====================================================
   STEP 1: POWER THE ESP32
=====================================================

  Use a Buck Converter to step down battery voltage
  to 3.3V or 5V for the ESP32.

  Battery/Charge Controller ---> Buck Converter
  Buck Converter OUT (+)    ---> ESP32 VIN / 5V pin
  Buck Converter OUT (-)    ---> ESP32 GND

=====================================================
   STEP 2: CONNECT ESP32 TO RELAY MODULE
=====================================================

  The relay module has 3 small pins on one side:

  Relay Pin     ESP32 Pin
  ---------     ---------
  VCC       --> 5V / VIN pin (Relay needs 5V to click)
  GND       --> GND
  IN        --> Any GPIO pin 5

  ⚠️ VOLTAGE NOTE: Even if your ESP32 is 3.3V, the relay
     module usually needs 5V on VCC to operate the coil.
     The ESP32's 3.3V signal on the IN pin is usually 
     enough to trigger it.

=====================================================
   STEP 3: CONNECT RELAY TO PUMP (via LOAD terminal)
=====================================================

  The relay module has 3 screw terminals:
    - COM  (Common)
    - NO   (Normally Open)   <-- USE THIS
    - NC   (Normally Closed)

  Wiring:
    Charge Controller LOAD (+) ---> Relay COM terminal
    Relay NO terminal          ---> Pump (+) wire
    Charge Controller LOAD (-) ---> Pump (-) wire (direct)

  TERMINAL GUIDE:
    - COM (Common): The power goes IN here.
    - NO (Normally Open): The power comes OUT when ESP says ON.
    - NC (Normally Closed): LEAVE EMPTY. If used, the pump 
      stays ON all the time and only turns OFF when ESP says ON.
    - It is designed for powering loads like pumps
    - Has built-in low-voltage disconnect protection
      (prevents battery from over-discharging)
    - Charge controller monitors the load current
    - No extra buck converter needed for 12V pump

  WHY USE NO (Normally Open)?
    - Pump stays OFF by default (safe)
    - Pump only turns ON when ESP32 activates relay

=====================================================
   HOW IT WORKS
=====================================================

  1. ESP32 sends HIGH signal to relay GPIO pin
     --> Relay closes the circuit
     --> Pump TURNS ON

  2. ESP32 sends LOW signal to relay GPIO pin
     --> Relay opens the circuit
     --> Pump TURNS OFF

  The relay only needs a few milliamps from ESP32.
  The pump draws its power from the LOAD terminal.
  They are electrically isolated.

=====================================================
   IMPORTANT TIPS
=====================================================

  1. Use a relay module with OPTOCOUPLER (most common
     blue SRD-05VDC modules have one). This protects
     the ESP32 from voltage spikes.

  2. If your pump draws MORE than 10A, use a MOSFET
     module (like IRF520) instead of a basic relay.

  3. Make sure your pump is rated for the same voltage
     as your battery (usually 12V).

  4. If using ESP8266 instead of ESP32, use pin D5 or
     D6 for relay control. AVOID D0, D3, D4, D8 as
     they can cause boot issues.

  5. If your pump is NOT 12V (different from battery
     voltage), THEN you need a buck converter for the
     pump too. But for 12V pump + 12V battery, the
     LOAD terminal is the simplest and safest option.

=====================================================
   QUICK SUMMARY
=====================================================

  Component     | Powered By              | Controlled By
  ------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------
  ESP32         | Buck Converter (3.3V)   | --
  Pump          | LOAD terminal (12V)     | Relay module
  Relay Module  | ESP32 (VCC + GND)       | ESP32 GPIO pin

  Total connections:
    - 2 wires: Buck converter to ESP32 (power)
    - 3 wires: ESP32 to relay (VCC, GND, signal)
    - 3 wires: LOAD terminal + relay to pump (power)
